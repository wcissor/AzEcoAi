The Problem

Roads don't warn you.
We built a system that does.

Ice forms silently. By the time a driver notices, it's too late. Traditional salting crews operate on schedules — not intelligence. They treat roads that are already dangerous, not roads that are about to become dangerous.

Existing weather stations are too sparse and too slow. Municipalities get yesterday's data when they need tomorrow's prediction. The entire paradigm is reactive when it needs to be proactive.

AzEco.Ai flips the model. We predict ice formation up to hours in advance using a trained ML model fed by distributed IoT sensors — then dispatch treatment units automatically, before the ice ever forms.

ML Engine

Random Forest
Ice Prediction Model

Trained on thousands of historical sensor readings, our Random Forest classifier uses 5 engineered features to predict ice formation with 94.3% accuracy.

  • 200 decision trees — ensemble for robustness against outliers
  • Dew point calculation — key physics-based ice formation indicator
  • Freeze Index — proprietary composite score combining temp × moisture
  • Flask REST API — sensor pellets call /predict_ice every 30 seconds
  • Real-time logging — all readings saved to sensor_log.csv for retraining

About AzEco.Ai

Built in Azerbaijan,
for Azerbaijan's roads

AzEco.Ai is an Azerbaijani deep-tech startup focused on using artificial intelligence to make public infrastructure safer and smarter. We started with road icing because it's a solvable problem that kills people every winter.

Our team combines expertise in machine learning, embedded systems, and urban infrastructure. We're building the software and hardware stack from the ground up — no black-box vendor solutions, full control of the prediction pipeline.

Our long-term vision: a city-wide environmental intelligence platform covering air quality, flood risk, and road conditions — all powered by the same sensor and ML infrastructure.
